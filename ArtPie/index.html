A surface player created for the reimaginers. 


Learn more about the project

For true music lovers only the best record players will do. While they sound fantastic, famous record players lack the art illusion and dynamic visuals which are keen to the eye. 

This is why we reimagined what a record player can be. Our project is an integrated mechanical and electrical system that uses computer vision to detect objects by shape and transform such into audiovisual scenes. 

Dynamic Art

Art tells stories which can be interpreted in many forms. This player tells stories through moving contours!

Audiovisual

The player generates personalized audio and light shows based on object geometry. 

Polish

Our clean product finish appeals to a wider audience.

Our Process

For this project, we completed a sequence of three two week-long sprints to design a mechatronic system. For each sprint, we completed an integrated system that progressively built out the final product. At the end of the each two week-long sprint, we held sprint reviews.

Meet Our Team

Zoie Leo

Zoie is passionate about the overlap of visual design and engineering. Throughout this project she learned about the wonderous world of Adobe Illustrator and how to force the program to collaborate with temperamental SolidWorks files. She is also interested in user interaction with form, specifically in the form of record players in this project. As before this project she was not a frequent user of record players.

Jasper Katzban

J​asper enjoys mixing sound and software together to design things that make us appreciate the physical environment in new ways. In this project, he was excited for the opportunity to learn about computer vision stacks, software systems, and integration across electrical, software, and mechanical domains. He thinks it was especially fun to write software that gives everyday objects their own sonic signature!

Priscila Morales

Outside of school, Priscila explores the world of numbers, design and music. As an avid lover of many things engineering, she had the opportunity to delve into the software and mechanical integration of the project. She was also enthusiastic to create this website as a way to share the team's passion with the world. With some lenses and keyboards, she captured the mundane, the extraordinary, and everything in between.

Aydin O'Leary

Although majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering, Aydin appreciates the value of a holistic approach and thus spent most of his time on system integration. He found it quite rewarding to make sure that everything fit together.

Makenzi Fischbach

Makenzi is an astrophysics major and a hopeful electrical and computer engineering major. She worked on the project's design as well as the LED implementation and integration.
